Introduction

Wangari Maathai was a Kenyan environmental activist, known for her relentless efforts in promoting sustainable development, democracy, and peace. She founded the Green Belt Movement, which focused on environmental conservation and women's rights. In 2004, she became the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize.

Achievements

Some of Wangari Maathai's notable achievements include:

  • Founding the Green Belt Movement, which has planted over 51 million trees in Kenya.
  • Advocating for women's rights and empowerment.
  • Receiving the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004 for her contributions to sustainable development, democracy, and peace.
